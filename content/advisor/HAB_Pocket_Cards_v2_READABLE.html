CARD 3 OF 4 — 8 BOSS BATTLES (FRONT, SHEET 2)
HAB · BATTLE REFERENCE
The 8 Boss Battles
Objections are uncertainty wearing a costume.
B1   “I'll just sell the car.” Reframe vs. used-car cost + unknown history.
B2   “Price too high.” “We're not cheapest, intentionally.” Anchor protection.
B3   “No money.” = “no money TODAY.” Pivot to financing. Stay on the line.
B4   “Need to talk to my spouse.” Three-way bypass. Get them on the call now.
B5   “I'll wait.” Safety: name the risk. Maint: value reframe.
B6   “You're always selling.” Pro Obligation frame. Fiduciary, not salesperson.
B7   “Need the car today.” Solve transport FIRST. Then return to the repair.
B8   “Parts & labor across the street?” Refuse apples-to-apples. Sell the protection program.

L.A.S.T. — The Recovery Combo

AACKNOWLEDGEemotion, no “but”

5 Universal Objection Rules

  1. Never argue. Win the argument, lose the customer.
  2. Acknowledge first, always. “I completely understand.”
  3. Find the real concern. Clarifying question, then respond.
  4. Pivot to value or risk — never to discount.
  5. Re-ask for the close. Every exchange ends moving forward.
FORBIDDEN WORDS “policy”  ·  “but”  ·  “however”  ·  “although”
Never discount before solving the real problem.
