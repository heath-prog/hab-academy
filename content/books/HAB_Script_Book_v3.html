Script 1 · Inbound Phone

The Full Inbound Call

Goal: book the appointment. Never quote a price on the phone.

[Pick up before the third ring. Smile before you speak.]

Advisor: "Thank you for calling [Shop]. My name is [Your Name]." [Pause. Let the customer speak.]

Customer: "Hi, do you work on brakes?"

Advisor: "You called the right place! Is this your first time with our shop?"

Customer: "Yes, it's my first time."

Advisor: "Thank you so much for calling us! We are [Shop], your certified dealer alternative, located at [address], open six days a week from [store hours]. We have ASE and dealer-certified technicians ready to handle all of your vehicle needs, from tires and service to major repairs. We stand behind our work with warranties up to 3 years or 36,000 miles. We also offer complimentary shuttles, vehicle pick-up and drop-off, ride services, rental car reimbursement, and loaner vehicles when available! What kind of concern are you having with your brakes?"

Customer: "They squeak when I stop."

Advisor: "Oh wow! WHEN you're experiencing squeaking like that, it's always best to bring your vehicle in and have it inspected by one of our certified technicians! Is now a good time?"

Customer: "I could come in this morning."

Advisor: "Great, stay with me on the line really quick while I gather your information to build a customer profile."

[Gather name, number, email, vehicle year, make, and model. Document the concern in the customer's own words.]

Advisor: "I have an opening at 9:15 or 10:45. Which works best for you?"

Customer: "9:15 works."

Advisor: "Perfect, [Customer Name], you're set for 9:15. We'll see you and your vehicle then!"

Variation · Returning caller

Customer: "I've been in before, I was just hoping for a quote."

Advisor: "I completely understand. What kind of concern are you having with your brakes?"

[Resume at the concern pivot above: "Oh wow! WHEN you're experiencing..."]

Script 3 · Inbound Phone · Price Counters

The Four Inbound Mini-Bosses

Counter, then close for the visit. Never a number over the phone.
Mini-Boss 1 · "Just tell me how much it will cost."

Customer: "Yeah, I just want to know how much it will cost before I come in."

Advisor: "AT NO COST TO YOU, I will personally inspect your vehicle alongside one of our certified technicians and provide you with a complete written estimate. When should we expect to see the vehicle?"

Mini-Boss 2 · "I already have a quote."

Customer: "I have a quote already from another shop. That's why I'm just looking for a price."

Advisor: "Oh, excellent! If you can bring that with you, that would be tremendously helpful! We have a Meet-or-Beat policy that allows us to price-match like-for-like quotes if we don't naturally beat the price with our estimate. To give you the most accurate pricing, though, it's best if our certified technician takes a look first to identify exactly what's needed to fix the [customer concern]. When should we expect to see the vehicle?"

Mini-Boss 3 · "Do you have a ballpark?"

Customer: "Do you have a ballpark for me? I just want to know roughly how much."

Advisor: "I completely understand! I will personally inspect your vehicle alongside one of our certified technicians and provide a complete written estimate so you can make informed decisions. If budget is your primary concern, I will work with you to select cost-effective solutions that still carry good warranty coverage on parts and labor. As far as a ballpark, I can pull up a tool we use called RepairPal and give you an idea of what something like this normally costs to repair."

Mini-Boss 4 · "Let me think about it."

Customer: "I'm not sure yet. Let me think about it."

Advisor: "I completely understand. Before you decide, may I ask what's most important to you: price, turnaround time, or overall service?" [Listen carefully.]

Every first-time customer. Walking pace, under 90 seconds, drink offer at the end.

We're excited to serve you today, and thank you for trusting [Shop]. Can I get you something to drink? Coffee, water, or tea?"

Script 6 · Phone · First Base

The PRS Write-Up Verbal (First Base Call)

Isolate the customer's original concern. Problem, Risk, Solution, Investment, Coverage — no pause before Coverage.

Advisor: "Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] with [Shop]. Is now a good time to review our findings?"

Customer: "Sure, go ahead."

Advisor: "You brought your vehicle in for [customer concern] and I reviewed the findings personally with the technician to ensure accuracy. Good news is I was able to capture a good video of what is going on. Did you receive the DVI findings I sent via text?"

Customer: "Yes, I saw them."

Advisor: "More good news, we have identified the cause of your concern! The problem is [Problem]. If it's left as it is, [Risk]. The proper solution is [Solution]. The total investment to take care of this today is $[XXX], and everything is covered by our 3-year, 36,000-mile nationwide warranty on parts and labor. While the vehicle is here we will also finish up the complete bumper-to-bumper inspection and will reach out with any additional concerns. Is this still the best number to reach you?"

Customer: "Yes it is."

Advisor: "Perfect. I'll get the parts ordered and have the technician get started right away."

[Answer questions. Overcome objections. Obtain authorization before advancing to second base. Hang up between bases.]

Script 7 · Phone · First Base Safety

The 10-Step ISO Presentation (Water Pump)

The canonical safety presentation. Structure never changes; only the technical content does.

Advisor: "Hi [Customer], this is [Advisor] from [Shop]. Thanks for your patience. Is now a good time to go over your vehicle?"

Customer: "Yes, now works."

Advisor: "I personally reviewed your vehicle with our certified technician so I could give you a clear picture of what's going on. Were you able to view the photos, videos, or the digital inspection report we sent over?"

Customer: "I did, yes."

Advisor: "You brought your vehicle in for an oil change. We've completed the initial inspection and I wanted to review what we found. The good news is your digital inspection report clearly documents everything with photos and notes so you can see exactly what we're seeing, and we caught this early, before it turned into a bigger issue."

Customer: "Okay."

Advisor: "More good news: during our inspection we found that your water pump is leaking, which is separate from your original visit and something we can take care of now before it leads to further damage. If left as is, a leaking water pump can lead to engine overheating, coolant loss, and eventually major engine damage. The proper repair is to remove and replace the water pump and complete a full cooling system service so everything is clean, sealed, and operating at the correct temperature. The total investment for today's work, including the cooling system service, is $1,695.46, parts and labor included, and this work is covered by our 3-year, 36,000-mile nationwide warranty on parts and labor."

Customer: "Alright."

Advisor: "While your vehicle is here, we'll finish the full bumper-to-bumper digital inspection and I'll reach out if anything else needs attention. Is this still the best number to reach you?"

Customer: "Yes, that number is best."

Advisor: "Perfect. I'll get everything moving and call you with an update."

Customer: "I did."

Advisor: "Good news is your vehicle's overall health is in EXCELLENT condition! The suspension system is operating properly. However, the axle boot is leaking grease. It's not an immediate failure, but left unaddressed it can damage the joint. The proper repair is to replace the CV axle to ensure proper lubrication and protection. To address all the concerns, the updated total investment is $[XXX], and obviously everything is covered by our 3-year, 36,000-mile nationwide warranty on both parts and labor. With the [safety or customer concern] in progress, I can go ahead and order these parts and have the technician take care of everything at once. Script 9 · Phone · Third Base

Third Base — Scheduled Maintenance Call

Soft sell. Calm tone, no urgency stacking. A real opt-out is part of the script.

Advisor: "Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] with [Shop], calling back with that maintenance plan. Is now a good time?"

Customer: "Sure."

Advisor: "The remaining items fall into preventative maintenance. Your transmission and differential fluids are overdue or coming due within the next 10,000 miles based on mileage, so ideally we should address those two services today while we have the car. Addressing them now keeps the systems up to date and maintains the lifetime protection plan, leaving the only services due next visit [services due], and the service after that will be the [plan]. Addressing the items due will move your total investment for today's work from $[XXX] to $[XXXX]. These services are covered by the lifetime protection plan that keeps major system components like the [expensive part] covered for unlimited miles, so long as the services don't go overdue. Can I have the tech get everything fully up to date while it's in the shop?"

Customer: "I'm not sure about doing all of it today."

Advisor: "No problem at all. We can absolutely schedule the services for your next visit so nothing goes overdue. Would you like me to set that up now so it's on the books?"

Script 10 · Objection

Boss Battle 1 — "I'm Selling the Car"

Reframe repair cost against the real alternative: buying someone else's unknown history.

Customer: "I appreciate everything, but I'm probably just going to sell it. I don't want to put more money into it."

Advisor: "I completely understand. Big repairs can make you step back and rethink things. Out of curiosity, are you planning to replace it with something newer or used?"

Customer: "Probably used. I don't want another big payment."

Advisor: "That makes sense. Let me give you something to consider. Most used vehicles today are still a major investment, and you're inheriting someone else's maintenance history. With your vehicle, we know exactly what condition it's in because we just inspected it bumper to bumper. Based on what we saw, this vehicle still has strong life left in it. Let me ask you this: if you could purchase this exact vehicle, knowing the major systems have just been inspected and repaired, for a fraction of the cost of another vehicle, would that make sense?"

Customer: "I didn't think about it that way."

Advisor: "Right now you're not throwing money at an old car. You're making a smart investment in a vehicle you already own and understand. The average used car today runs up to $25,000, and the insurance rates are outrageous. To get this vehicle back to safe and reliable, we're talking about [total investment]. I completely understand if you're simply in the market for a new car, but if you're looking to make a smart financial decision, my professional advice would be to repair the car instead of buying a used car that may need the same in repairs, or more."

Script 14 · Objection

Boss Battle 5 — "I'm Going to Wait"

Clarify risk if safety; reframe value if maintenance. Schedule whatever gets deferred.
Version 1 · Safety concern

Customer: "I think I'll wait on that."

Advisor: "I understand. I do want to be transparent. This one falls into a safety category. Right now the brakes are past normal wear and into potential damage. That can affect stopping distance and control. Are you planning to drive it?"

Customer: "Yes, just around town."

Advisor: "Even short trips carry risk in this condition. My job is to protect you, not pressure you. If budget is the concern, we can look at payment options, but I don't recommend postponing something that affects safety."

Version 2 · Preventative maintenance

Customer: "I'll just wait on the fluid services."

Advisor: "No worries, they're not urgent today. What we're seeing in those systems is buildup that doesn't show symptoms until damage starts. Doing them now protects the components while everything is accessible. If we skip them today, are you planning to schedule it soon, or wait until something starts acting up?"

Customer: "Probably the next oil change."

Advisor: "Understood. Just keep in mind preventative service is always less expensive than corrective repair. If it makes sense to bundle it while it's already here, we can space out the cost as well."

Script 18 · Recovery

L.A.S.T. Recovery — The Four Hot Counters

Listen fully before speaking. No "but." No "policy." Specific, time-bound, owned solve. Thank at the end.
Hot Counter 1 · No callback for two days

Customer: "My car's been here for two days and no one called me!"

Advisor: "Okay, walk me through it. When did you drop it off, and what were you expecting to hear?" [Listen. No interrupting.]

Advisor: "I completely understand. Not hearing back when you're waiting on your car is one of the most frustrating things, and that's on us to do better. Here's what I'm going to do. I'm pulling your RO right now. I'll get you the current status, a real ETA, and I'll call you back personally within 30 minutes whether the news is good or not. Thank you for letting me know. We'd much rather hear this from you than read about it later."

Hot Counter 2 · The bill is higher than the quote

Customer: "The bill is way more than what you quoted!"

Advisor: "Tell me what number you were expecting and what number you're seeing." [Listen fully.]

Advisor: "That's a fair concern. If you were planning on one number and the bill comes in higher, that breaks trust, and I get why you're frustrated. Let me pull the estimate and the final invoice side by side. I'll walk you line by line through what changed, and if any of that wasn't properly authorized, I'll make it right today. I appreciate you raising this instead of just walking away unhappy. Let's figure it out."

Hot Counter 3 · The noise came back

Customer: "I just paid for this last month and the same noise is back!"

Advisor: "Tell me about the noise. When does it happen, is it exactly the same, did it ever fully go away?" [Listen fully.]

Advisor: "That's exactly the kind of thing that should not be happening, and I understand why it feels like we let you down. Here's what we're going to do. We carry a 3-year, 36,000-mile warranty on parts and labor, so this is covered. I'm getting you on the schedule today or tomorrow, no charge, and we'll diagnose it personally. Can you bring it in this afternoon, or in the morning? And thank you for bringing it back to us instead of going somewhere else. We're going to make this right."

Hot Counter 4 · Grease on the seat

Customer: "Your tech left grease on my seat."

Advisor: "Can you show me?" [Walk to the car. Look. Do not minimize.]

Advisor: "You're right, that's not what should happen. That's on us. Here's what we're going to do. We use seat covers on every job and that wasn't followed today. I'm going to get this professionally detailed at our cost and have your car back to you cleaner than when it came in. I'll arrange a loaner or an Uber so you're not without a ride. When works best? And thank you for telling me directly. This is how we get better, one customer at a time."

Script 20 · In Person · Checkout

Home Plate — Booking the Next Visit

Final invoice review. Reinforce value, direction-facing close, next visit on the books before they leave.

Advisor: "[Mr./Mrs. Customer], thank you again for trusting us with your vehicle today. Here's what we completed: [services]. Everything is digitally documented in your invoice. Your total investment today is $[XXX]. Which direction would you like your vehicle facing when we bring it up?"

Customer: "Facing out would be great."

Advisor: "You got it. Before you head out, a few benefits included with today's service: our 3-year, 36,000-mile nationwide warranty, 365-day roadside assistance, a post-repair inspection, and maintenance reminders. Looking at your maintenance schedule, your next service will be due around [date or mileage]. Let's get that on the books now so it's one less thing to think about.
